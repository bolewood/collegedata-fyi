# Gemini Deep Research Prompt: Common Data Set Ecosystem

Copy everything below the line into Gemini Deep Research.

---

I'm building an open-source project that collects and serves Common Data Set (CDS) information from US colleges and universities. I need a comprehensive research report covering the following areas. Please be thorough and cite specific sources, institutions, and data points wherever possible.

## 1. What is the Common Data Set and who runs it?

- What is the Common Data Set Initiative? Who founded it, when, and why?
- Which organizations are the current partners/sponsors (College Board, Peterson's, US News & World Report — are there others)?
- Is there a formal governing body, or is it an informal consortium?
- Where does the official CDS template live (commondataset.org)? Who maintains the website and template files?
- How has the template format changed over time? When was the most recent revision and what changed?
- Is there any indication the CDS Initiative is winding down, expanding, or being replaced by something else?

## 2. Which schools participate and which don't?

- How many US colleges/universities currently publish a CDS? Is there an authoritative count anywhere?
- Is participation voluntary or required? Does any accreditor, ranking system, or federal body mandate it?
- What types of institutions are most likely to participate: 4-year vs 2-year, public vs private, large vs small, selective vs open-admission?
- Which notable institutions are known to NOT publish a CDS? Are there patterns (e.g., for-profit schools, community colleges, certain elite institutions)?
- Do any schools publish CDS data in a format other than PDF (e.g., HTML pages, Excel, interactive dashboards)?
- How do schools typically make their CDS available — on their institutional research website, a specific URL pattern, or through a central repository?

## 3. What's the incentive for schools to participate?

- What do schools get out of filling out the CDS? Is it primarily about US News rankings eligibility?
- Does US News require CDS submission, or do they collect data through a separate survey that overlaps with CDS?
- Does Peterson's require or incentivize CDS submission? What does Peterson's do with the data?
- How does CDS relate to IPEDS (the federal Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System)? Is there significant overlap in the data fields? Do schools typically fill out both?
- Are there schools that have publicly refused to participate in the CDS or stopped participating? Why?
- Has the "rankings boycott" movement (e.g., law schools withdrawing from US News) affected CDS participation?

## 4. How is CDS data currently consumed?

- Who are the main consumers of published CDS data: students, parents, counselors, researchers, journalists, ranking systems?
- Are there any existing tools, databases, or APIs that aggregate CDS data across multiple schools? (Not IPEDS — I know about that. I'm asking specifically about CDS aggregation.)
- Do any commercial products (CollegeBoard BigFuture, Niche, College Confidential, Naviance, etc.) use CDS data, and if so, how do they obtain it?
- Is there academic research that relies on CDS data specifically (as opposed to IPEDS)?

## 5. Discovery and accessibility challenges

- I'm finding it difficult to locate CDS PDFs for smaller schools. Is there a central repository or index of all published CDS files?
- What are the common URL patterns where schools publish their CDS (e.g., institutional research pages)?
- Do schools tend to keep historical CDS files available, or do they only post the current year?
- Are there accessibility (WCAG) requirements for publicly posted CDS documents? Are schools struggling with this?
- Has anyone attempted a comprehensive web scrape or collection of all published CDS PDFs?

## 6. The future of the CDS

- Is there any discussion in higher education circles about modernizing the CDS format (e.g., moving to structured data, APIs, or machine-readable formats)?
- How does the CDS relate to newer transparency initiatives (College Scorecard, Gainful Employment, the FAFSA Simplification Act)?
- Are there any known competitors or alternatives to the CDS that serve a similar purpose?
- What is the general sentiment among institutional researchers about the CDS — do they find it valuable, burdensome, or both?

## Format preferences

- Please organize findings by section as numbered above
- Include specific school names and examples wherever possible
- Flag any claims where your confidence is low or sources conflict
- If you find any existing datasets, spreadsheets, or lists of "schools that publish CDS" with URLs, please include those links
- I'm especially interested in anything about the *difficulty of finding CDS files for smaller schools* since that directly affects my project's feasibility
