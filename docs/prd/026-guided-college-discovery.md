## Problem Statement

The current `/browse` and `/match` experiences begin with selectivity, test scores,
and filters. They are useful after a family already knows which schools to consider,
but they do little to help a high school student discover credible schools outside
their existing mental map.

The new experience serves a student who has several interests but no settled major.
It helps that student learn what they value, translate interests into possible
academic paths, and discover unfamiliar schools with inspectable reasons. Parents and
counselors may review the result later, but the student is the protagonist.

This is a public-interest, open-source product. It must not optimize recommendations
for recruitment relationships, affiliate revenue, or institutional promotion.

## What Makes This Cool

Most college-search products ask students to declare their preferences before showing
them concrete possibilities. This product assumes the reverse: teenagers often learn
their preferences by reacting to examples.

The opening combines two interaction ideas:

1. A mixed card sort gives the student concrete language for academic, geographic,
   community, and opportunity preferences.
2. An AI-assisted reflection interprets those choices, asks one or two high-value
   follow-ups, and hands an editable preference model to a deterministic discovery
   engine.

The engine returns small, deliberately varied discovery rounds rather than a ranked
list. Every school explains why it appeared and names the supporting evidence. The
magical moment is: “This school was not on my radar, but the reasons make sense.”

## Constraints

### Product constraints

- Student-led language and defaults. Do not begin with GPA, test scores, acceptance
  rates, or “chance me.”
- No visible universal fit score or program-quality score.
- A discovery round is not an application list and must never be labeled as one.
- Program strength is an evidence bundle, not a claim about prestige, teaching
  quality, or departmental superiority.
- The AI may interpret, ask, and explain. It may not silently select or rank schools.
- All inferred preferences remain visible, editable, and attributable to their source.
- Every recommendation reason must resolve to current structured evidence or clearly
  labeled editorial context stored as a versioned evidence record. Unstored model
  knowledge and ad hoc prose can never support a recommendation reason.
- The first prototype must be usable without creating an account.
- The research prototype and production v1 are separate milestones. The prototype may
  use a curated fixture and scripted reflection; production v1 uses live evidence and
  a schema-constrained model.

### Geography constraints

- “Geographic boundaries and preferences” appears before the card sort because
  distance may be a family boundary,
  not a preference the student is still discovering.
- The student may provide an optional home ZIP, preferred radius, and absolute maximum
  radius.
- The engine may surface a clearly labeled geographic wildcard beyond the preferred
  radius. It must never violate the absolute maximum.
- Distances are approximate great-circle miles, not driving time. The UI says so.
- The ZIP is used for the current discovery session and is not persisted server-side
  by default.
- Geographic experience cards remain separate: city versus college town, climate,
  walkability, airport access, outdoor recreation, and similar qualities answer a
  different question than radial distance.

### Data constraints

- Current CDS data is institution-wide and cannot establish program-level admissions
  competitiveness.
- IPEDS completions show awards conferred, not enrollment demand, student satisfaction,
  curriculum quality, faculty quality, or selectivity within a major.
- A zero completion count may mean a new, renamed, reorganized, or very small program.
  It must not automatically mean “program unavailable.”
- Production v1 does not claim that a program is currently offered. Its narrow claim is
  “this institution reported recent bachelor’s awards in this field.” Current-offering
  verification is deferred until an authoritative source is added.
- College Scorecard field-of-study outcomes are often aggregated to four-digit CIP,
  suppressed for privacy, or unavailable for small programs. Missing outcomes must
  remain visibly missing.
- Program, geography, cost, and admissions data have different reporting years. Every
  evidence panel must display source and vintage.
- The existing `institution_directory` already carries latitude and longitude. The
  current IPEDS projection includes locale but not six-digit completions by program.

### Accessibility constraints

- Dragging is optional. Every card has visible tap/click controls and full keyboard
  operation.
- Sorting a card announces its new bucket to assistive technology.
- Buckets, progress, and inferred themes are not communicated by color alone.
- The card sort works at 320 CSS pixels and 400% zoom.
- Reduced-motion users receive no animated card flight or spatial reordering.
- Natural-language reflection has a non-AI fallback: the student can accept, edit, or
  skip the reflection and continue with card-derived preferences.

### First-release scope boundaries

Production v1 is one reviewed academic-interest lake, not a national answer for every
major. It includes:

- optional distance boundary and preference
- one 20–24 card mixed deck
- deterministic card-to-preference mapping
- at most two AI reflection questions, with a no-AI path
- recent-award evidence for one related CIP family
- one reproducible six-school discovery round at a time
- reactions and a local research-next shelf

Production v1 explicitly excludes:

- arbitrary-major coverage or full CIP-universe claims
- institutional-page scraping or editorial evidence collection
- algorithmic “familiarity” or prestige estimation
- personalized admissions chances, application-list balancing, or aid prediction
- accounts, cross-device sync, share codes, print, and export
- public discovery APIs or stability guarantees
- AI-authored recommendation facts
- program trends until CIP crosswalks and longitudinal QA are complete

Policy vocabulary:

- **Reviewed ontology edge:** a versioned edge with `review_status=approved`, reviewer,
  review date, explanation, and matching CIP taxonomy version.
- **Supported preference:** a nonconflicted active signal whose preference key has a
  `discovery_policy_v1` evidence matcher and at least one usable evidence fact for the
  candidate.
- **Recent award evidence:** a positive bachelor’s completion count in the production
  release’s single named IPEDS collection cycle. “Recent” always displays that year.
- **In scope:** the exact eligibility predicate in `discovery_policy_v1`, not an
  editorial judgment.
- **Verified evidence:** a successfully loaded fact that passes schema, identity,
  source-vintage, and quality checks. It does not mean independently audited truth.

## Premises

1. The product’s job is preference discovery, not school ranking.
2. Small, deliberately varied rounds teach more than one long results table.
3. “Strong program” is an evidence bundle, never a proprietary quality score.
4. AI interprets and explains; deterministic data selects candidates.
5. Academic positioning is a downstream list-balancing layer, not the opening frame.
6. Hard constraints and preferences are different data types. A recommendation may
   challenge a preference but must not violate a hard constraint.
7. Reactions to real schools are new preference evidence, not merely engagement data.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Transparent Explorer

Expand `/browse` with interest, program, geography, size, and environment filters.
Add evidence-backed “why this appeared” cards and optional free-text parsing.

This is the lowest-risk improvement and reuses the current query model. It was not
chosen because it still requires the student to know and declare preferences before
seeing useful examples, making it a stronger version of the established filter-and-
match pattern rather than a different discovery model.

### Approach B: Iterative Discovery Guide

Use natural language plus an editable preference model to generate rounds of six
schools. Student reactions refine subsequent rounds. The candidate engine remains
deterministic and every explanation is grounded in retrieved evidence.

This is the strongest complete architecture, but beginning with an open-ended prompt
creates blank-page anxiety and asks a teenager to articulate preferences they may not
yet understand.

### Approach C: College Tasting Flight

Begin with choices between concrete experiences and tradeoffs, then reveal contrasting
schools. This reduces abstract questioning and creates an immediately delightful
interaction.

This was not selected alone because pure pairwise choices can manufacture false
opposites and a game-like experience does not create enough room for nuance.

## Recommended Approach

Build a hybrid of B and C: **geographic boundaries → mixed card sort → AI reflection
→ iterative evidence-backed discovery rounds → research-next shelf → later list
balance**.

### 1. Optional geographic boundaries and preferences

Before card-based preference discovery, ask the distance boundary and soft preference:

- Starting ZIP
- “Prefer within approximately ___ miles”
- “Never beyond approximately ___ miles”
- “Show an occasional geographic wildcard beyond my preferred distance”

Both radii are optional. If preferred is greater than maximum, show an inline error.
If the home ZIP cannot be resolved, explain the problem and let the student correct it
or continue without distance settings. Production v1 has no state/region fallback.

### 2. Mixed experience-card sort

Present 20–24 cards drawn from a larger versioned library. The student assigns each
card to one of four buckets:

- Essential
- Interesting
- Not important
- Not for me

Cards mix domains so the student must prioritize across them. Each card describes an
experience rather than a metric.

#### Initial card domains

| Domain | Example experience | Candidate evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Academic flexibility | I can explore several related subjects before choosing a major. | CIP breadth, adjacent-program graph, undergraduate structure |
| Faculty access | Professors are likely to know me personally. | institution scale, student-faculty ratio, undergraduate orientation; label as proxies |
| Experiential learning | Undergraduates can join meaningful research early. | reflection-only in v1; verified institutional evidence would be required later |
| Field learning | Outdoor or community fieldwork can be part of ordinary class life. | program and curricular sources; initially editorial/verified |
| Place | I can walk from campus into a lively town. | NCES locale, coordinates, external place data if later added |
| City access | I can reach a major city without living in one. | coordinates and metro proximity |
| Residential life | Most students live on or near campus. | CDS F fields and IPEDS housing context |
| Scale | I want many specialized courses and organizations to choose from. | enrollment, CIP breadth, institutional type |
| Community | School spirit and big athletic events shape campus life. | athletic division and institution characteristics; no value judgment |
| Diversity | I want classmates with many different backgrounds and experiences. | enrollment composition with careful definitions and source dates |
| Affordability | I want schools where the reported aid picture is worth investigating. | merit profile, net price by income, grant share; never a personal award prediction |
| Outcomes | I care about evidence that students persist and complete. | retention, completion, outcome measures; cohort caveats visible |

Every card definition includes:

- stable `card_id`
- version
- domain
- student-facing statement
- plain-language explanation
- preference keys affected
- evidence keys that can support the preference
- limitations copy

Cards with weak or unavailable evidence may still help the reflection, but they cannot
independently generate a school recommendation reason.

### 3. Inspectable preference model

The sort produces structured preferences before AI runs.

```text
PreferenceSignal
  signal_id
  key: string
  domain: academic | place | community | opportunity | affordability | outcomes
  direction: seek | avoid | neutral
  strength: essential | interesting | low
  magnitude: 1 | 3
  source: card | reflection | school_reaction | explicit_edit
  source_id: card_id, prompt_id, or school_id
  confidence: explicit | inferred
  active: boolean
  supersedes_signal_ids[]
  reason_evidence_ids[]
  override_value: integer[-5,5] | null
  created_at: session-relative timestamp
```

#### Deterministic card mapping

All opening cards are phrased as positive experiences. Their buckets map as follows:

| Bucket | Signal written | Base weight |
|---|---|---:|
| Essential | `direction=seek`, `strength=essential`, `confidence=explicit` | +3 |
| Interesting | `direction=seek`, `strength=interesting`, `confidence=explicit` | +1 |
| Not important | no preference signal; retain the card response for UI state only | 0 |
| Not for me | `direction=avoid`, `strength=essential`, `confidence=explicit` | -3 |

A card may map to multiple preference keys, but each mapping is versioned in the card
definition. For a given preference key, sum active card weights and clamp the internal
total to `[-5, +5]`. Store the constituent signals; never store only the total.

Aggregate active, nonsuperseded signals for a key. Card signals use magnitude 3 or 1
from the table above. An accepted reflection signal starts at magnitude 1 and can be
promoted by the student to 3. A school reaction contributes magnitude 1. Only one active
reaction signal may exist per `(school_id, preference_key)`; a changed reaction
supersedes the prior one. Sum signed magnitudes and clamp to `[-5,+5]`.

An explicit ledger edit writes an `explicit_edit` signal with `override_value` and
supersedes every active signal for that key without deleting history. A later explicit
edit supersedes the earlier edit. Supersession is effective only while the superseding
signal is active. Deletion sets the edit inactive, so underlying signals become
effective again unless another active signal supersedes them. AI-proposed signals do not exist in session
state or affect candidates until the student accepts them.

If active explicit card signals for the same key include both seek and avoid weights,
mark the key `conflicted`. Before the first discovery round, present the exact cards
that caused the conflict and offer `Prefer`, `Avoid`, or `Leave open`. If the student
leaves it open or skips reflection, the key contributes zero to candidate relevance
but remains visible. This is the deterministic no-AI resolution path.

Apply the same conflict rule across every active, nonsuperseded directional signal,
including accepted reflection and school reactions. An explicit edit resolves the
conflict for that key while active. Opposing signals never disappear merely because
their numeric sum is zero.

#### Hard-constraint model

Hard constraints never share the preference-signal table.

```text
DiscoveryConstraint
  constraint_id
  kind: maximum_distance | excluded_state | institution_control (future)
  operator: lte | not_in | equals
  value
  unit
  source: explicit_form | explicit_conversion
  confirmed: boolean
  policy_version
  created_at: session-relative timestamp
```

Only `confirmed=true` constraints enter eligibility filtering. Cards cannot create a
constraint. The UI may ask whether an “Essential” or “Not for me” signal should become
a hard constraint later, but the default remains a preference.

Soft distance settings use a separate structure:

```text
GeographyPreference
  origin_lat_rounded
  origin_lon_rounded
  origin_source_version
  preferred_miles: integer | null
  allow_wildcards: boolean
```

When preferred radius exists without a maximum, a wildcard means any eligible school
outside the preferred radius. When no preferred radius exists, wildcard permission is
ignored. The UI explains this before continuing.

#### Geography enforcement

- Resolve a valid five-digit ZIP to a versioned centroid record and immediately derive
  origin coordinates. The session stores the ZIP locally; server requests receive only
  derived coordinates rounded to three decimals, sufficient for approximate distance.
- If ZIP resolution fails, distance settings remain disabled until the ZIP is corrected.
  The student may continue without geographic origin, preference, or constraint.
- If a school lacks coordinates and a maximum radius is active, exclude it from the
  candidate pool with diagnostic reason `missing_coordinates_under_hard_radius`.
- If no maximum is active, a coordinate-less school remains eligible but cannot fill a
  distance-dependent slot or reason.
- Preferred radius is a soft preference worth a documented contribution. Maximum
  radius is an invariant tested independently of relevance.

#### Preference rules

- Explicit edits override inferred signals.
- An “Essential” card does not become a hard constraint unless the UI explicitly asks
  and the student confirms that conversion.
- Contradictory preferences stay visible and contribute zero until the student resolves
  them. Reflection may explain the tension but is never the required resolver.
- The UI shows a plain-language preference ledger at all times.
- Students can delete a signal, reduce its importance, or mark the system’s inference
  wrong.

### 4. AI reflection

After the card sort, ask:

> What subjects, questions, or problems keep pulling you back? A few words are enough.

Offer reviewed starter concepts from the v1 lake as optional chips, but never require
one. This is the first natural-language step, so the student is responding after they
have concrete preference language rather than facing a blank opening screen.

Production v1 is visibly labeled as a pilot for its single reviewed interest family.
If the answer maps only outside that family, show the nearby concepts the system
understood and explain that evidence-backed school discovery for that interest is not
ready. The student may revise the interest, continue to the general nonpersonalized
`/browse` surface, or end the session. The engine must not substitute the supported lake
or return school recommendations under an unsupported interest.

The no-AI path normalizes the answer and matches only reviewed exact labels and aliases
in `InterestConcept`. One match selects that concept after student confirmation;
multiple matches require the student to choose; no match displays the reviewed concept
chips. The student must select a supported concept or exit to `/browse`. Free text never
maps directly to CIP codes without a reviewed alias or the validated AI proposal path.

The model receives only:

- selected card definitions and buckets
- geography summary, with ZIP removed after distance features are derived where
  feasible
- current structured preference signals
- the student’s current free-text answer
- a bounded taxonomy of academic paths and place concepts

It returns schema-validated JSON containing:

- a two- or three-sentence reflection
- at most two tensions or ambiguities
- one high-value follow-up question at a time
- proposed new preference signals with confidence `inferred`
- several possible academic-path concepts, never a single prescribed major

The model does **not** receive the school catalog during reflection and does not return
school IDs. This prevents conversational fluency from becoming an unreviewed candidate
selector.

The student reviews proposed signals before discovery. “Continue without AI” remains
available.

AI policy for production v1:

- Maximum two follow-up questions. The student may skip either and start discovery.
- Student free text is limited to 1,000 characters per response and treated as
  untrusted data, never as system or tool instructions.
- Output is accepted only when every preference key and concept ID exists in the
  versioned reviewed taxonomy. Unknown IDs, school IDs, URLs, and unsupported fields
  fail validation.
- On invalid output, retry once with the validation errors. A second failure activates
  the deterministic card-only path.
- Record model name, model snapshot/version, prompt-policy version, taxonomy version,
  and validation outcome in privacy-safe request diagnostics. Do not log free text.
- The model cannot create taxonomy aliases, evidence, hard constraints, or candidate
  weights.
- Before sending text, disclose the model provider, purpose, transport, provider
  retention setting, and that the text is optional. Production enables model processing
  for minors only with a provider configuration contractually/API-configured for no
  training and zero or minimum operational retention. If that posture cannot be
  verified, ship card-only reflection.
- Crisis, self-harm, abuse, or other safety-sensitive text receives a neutral safety
  response and appropriate public resources; it is not converted into college
  preferences.

### 5. Academic-interest graph

Create a versioned graph that connects student language to official CIP program codes.
It is not an embedding-only black box.

```text
InterestConcept
  concept_id
  label
  aliases[]
  taxonomy_version

InterestEdge
  edge_id
  from_concept_id
  to_concept_id_or_cip
  target_type: concept | cip
  relationship: direct | adjacent | exploratory
  credential_levels[]
  explanation
  reviewer
  review_status
  reviewed_at
  cip_taxonomy_version
```

Example: “climate + chemistry + public policy” may map to environmental chemistry,
environmental science, geoscience, environmental engineering, public policy,
environmental economics, and interdisciplinary environmental studies. The UI explains
these as possible paths and lets the student remove any branch.

Start with a reviewed ontology covering the prototype’s interest family. Expand by
reviewed lakes only after production-v1 validation. Log unmapped concept IDs and
aggregate failure counts, never raw student language, to a review queue.

### 6. Program-evidence layer

Add a public serving layer separate from the existing CDS browser row.

Use normalized facts so mixed sources never share one ambiguous vintage.

```text
ProgramEvidenceFact
  evidence_id
  ipeds_unitid
  school_id
  cip_code
  cip_taxonomy_version
  cip_level: 2 | 4 | 6
  credential_level
  metric: bachelor_completions | institution_completion_share |
          related_program_count | median_earnings | median_debt
  value_numeric
  unit
  data_year
  collection_year
  source_dataset
  source_table
  source_variable
  source_url
  release_type
  loaded_at
  verified_at
  quality_flags[]

ProgramEvidenceSummary
  ipeds_unitid
  school_id
  concept_id
  ontology_version
  direct_recent_award_count
  adjacent_recent_award_count
  related_cip_count
  outcome_fact_ids[]
  evidence_fact_ids[]

```

All recommendation facts, including existing directory/CDS/Scorecard facts, implement
a common reference envelope:

```text
EvidenceRef
  evidence_id
  school_id
  ipeds_unitid
  namespace: program | directory | distance | price | aid | admissions | outcomes
  metric
  value
  unit
  data_year
  source_release_id
  source_manifest_checksum
  immutable_fact_version
  source_table
  source_variable
  source_url
  derived_method_version: nullable
  quality_flags[]
```

Existing serving rows receive adapter-generated stable evidence IDs based on source
release, manifest checksum or immutable fact version, school, metric, and data year.
An upstream correction therefore creates a new ID rather than changing evidence behind
an old manifest. Derived distance creates a session-scoped
evidence ID from rounded origin, school coordinates, centroid-data version, and
distance-method version; neither the ID nor round diagnostics contain ZIP.

Unavailability is an addressable coverage record, not a zero-valued fact:

```text
EvidenceCoverage
  coverage_id
  school_id
  namespace
  metric
  concept_id: nullable
  cip_code: nullable
  credential_level: nullable
  cohort: nullable
  data_year
  source_release_id
  status: available | suppressed | not_collected | not_loaded | unknown
  explanation
```

Inputs:

- IPEDS `CYYYY_A` completions by six-digit CIP and award level
- College Scorecard field-of-study rows at four-digit CIP and credential level
- existing `institution_directory` coordinates and directory attributes
- existing `school_browser_rows`, `school_merit_profile`, and federal facts

Evidence claims are deliberately narrow:

- “Reported bachelor’s awards in this field during [year]…”
- “This subject accounts for a relatively large share of recent awards…”
- “Reported bachelor’s awards across several adjacent fields in [year]…”
- “Federal field-level outcomes are available/unavailable…”

Never translate those into “top,” “best,” “elite,” or “high quality.”

Production-v1 academic eligibility requires at least one positive bachelor’s award in
the direct or adjacent CIP set during the selected IPEDS completion cycle. A zero or
missing count is unknown, never negative evidence. Schools without positive recent-
award evidence do not enter the one-lake v1 pool, and the UI describes the resulting
coverage limitation. Current program-offering claims and longitudinal trends are
deferred.

The loader accepts a fact only when its CIP taxonomy version matches the ontology
edge’s version. A reviewed crosswalk may normalize a mismatched fact and must emit the
crosswalk ID and confidence flag. Without an approved crosswalk, reject the fact from
candidate evidence rather than matching codes by string coincidence.

Source manifests preserve the official release ID, downloaded artifact checksum,
retrieval timestamp, and local raw-table checksum. URLs are navigation metadata, not
the sole reproducibility mechanism.

Identity joins use canonical field name `ipeds_unitid` for the federal `UNITID` value.
An adapter maps the existing directory’s `ipeds_id` column into that canonical field.
The data spike must report unmatched IDs,
branch campuses, multi-campus systems, closures, consolidations, and one-to-many joins.
Ambiguous joins are excluded from production-v1 program recommendations until reviewed.

### 7. Deterministic candidate pipeline

Candidate generation is governed by a versioned `discovery_policy_v1`. Given identical
evidence versions, ontology version, policy version, constraints, and preferences, it
must return identical ordered rounds.

Candidate generation occurs in ordered stages:

1. **Eligibility:** active Title-IV, primarily bachelor’s-or-above, public or private
   nonprofit institutions present in `institution_directory`, with an unambiguous
   `ipeds_unitid` mapping. Withdrawn and verified-absent public-participation rows remain
   excluded under existing project policy.
2. **Hard constraints:** absolute distance maximum and any later explicitly confirmed
   hard exclusions.
3. **Academic plausibility:** at least one positive bachelor’s award in the selected
   cycle for a direct or adjacent CIP edge in the v1 interest lake.
4. **Preference relevance:** sum only supported, nonconflicted signals:
   - academic match is awarded once per candidate: +6 when any selected concept has a
     direct reviewed edge with qualifying evidence; otherwise +3 when at least one has
     an adjacent reviewed edge; multiple edges do not stack
   - for each supported preference key, apply its clamped `[-5,+5]` aggregate once,
     multiplied by a
     versioned evidence matcher returning `-1`, `0`, or `+1`
   - inside preferred radius: +2; outside preferred radius: 0
   - unsupported/reflection-only card signals: 0 for selection, still visible in the
     ledger
5. **Round composition:** choose a varied set through explicit slots rather than the
   six highest scalar scores.
6. **Explanation assembly:** attach fact IDs and versioned templates. Production v1
   uses template-grounded language; freeform AI rewriting is post-validation.

The internal relevance score is inspectable in logs and tests but never shown as a
universal fit percentage.

Every preference key in `discovery_policy_v1` defines its evidence matcher and
thresholds. Matcher `+1` means verified evidence aligns with the positive statement;
`-1` means verified evidence supports its defined opposite; `0` means unknown,
unsupported, missing, suppressed, or inside a neutral band. Absence never means
mismatch. Because avoid signals carry a negative aggregate, a `+1` match to the avoided
quality reduces relevance and a verified `-1` opposite increases it.

Tie-break candidates by, in order: more direct evidence facts, more supported
preference dimensions, fewer quality flags, then `school_id` ascending. This final
stable key guarantees reproducibility.

### 8. Discovery-round composition

Default round size: six schools. On narrow/mobile layouts, show one card at a time but
retain the round as a set.

Target slots, filled in order without reusing a school:

1. **Anchor:** strongest direct evidence match.
2. **Flexible path:** highest related-CIP count among remaining direct matches.
3. **Contrast:** highest academic relevance among schools that mismatch exactly one
   supported `interesting` preference and no `essential` seek/avoid signal.
4. **Affordability context:** lowest nonmissing `avg_net_price` from the single named
   Scorecard release among remaining academically plausible schools. The normative
   `discovery_policy_v1` field is `scorecard_summary.avg_net_price`, projected from
   Scorecard `NPT4_PUB` for public institutions and `NPT4_PRIV` for private institutions.
   Display the
   federal metric’s population and year, and never call it the student’s price or an aid
   prediction. Missing values cannot fill this slot. Tie-break with normal candidate
   ordering.
5. **Geographic wildcard:** highest relevance outside preferred radius and, when a
   maximum exists, inside that maximum; only when allowed.
6. **Exploration:** highest remaining relevance after diversity constraints.

If a slot is unavailable, skip it; do not weaken its definition. After attempting all
six slots, fill remaining positions from overall relevance with role `Additional
exploration` only when the school passes every hard and academic gate. If fewer than
four schools remain, return the smaller
round with a diagnostic explaining the limiting constraint.

Round-level constraints allow at most two schools from one state and at most three from
one control type when the eligible pool contains alternatives. Apply these during each
slot selection. If they prevent a four-school minimum, relax control type first, then
state, and record the relaxation in round diagnostics. There is no selectivity-band or
prestige/familiarity constraint in v1. Protected-class data must not be used to infer
the student’s identity or silently personalize selection.

Each round stores a diagnostic manifest: input policy/data versions, eligible and
excluded counts by reason, slot winner and runner-up IDs, diversity rejections,
relaxations, evidence IDs used, active constraint IDs, a normalized preference-state
hash, and the complete `GeographyPreference` excluding ZIP. It excludes ZIP and free
text.

Cross-round policy:

- schools on the research-next shelf never repeat
- schools marked `Not for me` never repeat in the current session
- other shown schools receive a two-round cooldown
- if fewer than four candidates remain after cooldowns, re-admit the oldest unreacted
  schools only; never re-admit saved or rejected schools
- a re-admitted card is labeled `Revisit` and states why the constrained pool is small
- preference or constraint changes trigger a new round with a new manifest; they do not
  mutate prior manifests

### 9. School discovery card

Each card contains:

- school name and location
- round role such as “Flexible path” or “Geographic wildcard”
- two or three reasons it appeared
- evidence class, reporting year, and expandable source details for each reason
- visible caveat when the reason relies on a proxy
- approximate distance from origin when provided
- actions: `Research next`, `More like this`, `Not for me`, `Wrong reason`, and
  `Tell me more`

There is no overall rank, match percentage, or “best fit” label.

Every displayed reason is a stored, testable object:

```text
RecommendationReason
  reason_id
```

### 10. Reaction semantics

Reactions have distinct meanings:

- `Research next` requires the student to choose one displayed recommendation reason as
  the saved reason, then asks “Was this school already familiar to you?” (`yes`, `name
  only`, `no`). An optional short personal note remains local. Saving does not alter
  preferences.
- `More like this` requires the student to select one displayed reason before writing a
  `school_reaction` seek signal of +1 for that reason’s preference key.
- `Not for me` offers displayed reasons plus “something else.” Selecting a displayed
  reason writes a -1 signal for only that preference key; skipping writes no signal.
- `Wrong reason` opens the fact and limitation details. `Preference is wrong` writes an
  explicit ledger edit after confirmation. `Evidence looks wrong` adds the evidence ID
  and reason code to a data-quality queue but does not change global evidence
  automatically.
- Skipping a card is not a negative preference.

Student-declared familiarity at `Research next` is the only v1 familiarity measure. It
is not inferred from traffic, prestige, search popularity, or institutional marketing.

The next round shows a short change note: “This round includes more interdisciplinary
programs and fewer isolated settings because of your last choices.”

### 11. Research-next shelf and downstream handoff

The shelf holds promising schools with the student’s own saved reason. It supports:

- comparison by the evidence dimensions that caused discovery
- direct source review
- explicit transition into cost review and academic positioning
- a later, out-of-v1 list-balance view across geography, affordability, and admissions
  outlook

The existing `/match` logic may power the academic-positioning step after discovery,
but its ranking order must not leak backward into candidate discovery.

Production v1 stores the shelf locally but does not ship sharing, printing, exporting,
or cross-device sync.

### 12. Privacy and safety

- Default session state stays in the browser.
- No account, full name, school name, exact address, demographic profile, transcript,
  or essay is required.
- ZIP is PII-adjacent. Do not include it in analytics or AI prompts when a derived
  geographic representation is sufficient.
- Analytics record aggregate interaction counts and card IDs, never free text or ZIP.
- Free-text retention defaults to session-only. Any future server retention requires
  a separate privacy review and explicit consent.
- Do not infer protected traits or use them in candidate ranking.
- The UI repeatedly describes recommendations as research suggestions, not counseling,
  admissions predictions, or guarantees.

#### Student-research protocol

For prototype sessions with minors:

- obtain parent/guardian permission and student assent using a short plain-language form
- explain that the prototype is research, not admissions counseling
- do not collect full name, transcript, essay, demographic profile, or exact address
- current college lists use school IDs/names only and are deleted with session notes
  after synthesis
- screen/audio recording is off by default; any recording needs separate explicit
  permission and a stated deletion date
- observers take task-level notes, not judgments about the student
- students may stop or remove their notes at any time
- do not use research sessions to train a model

### 13. Accessibility acceptance

Production v1 must verify:

- complete card sorting and bucket revision using keyboard alone, with logical focus
  order and no drag operation
- after a card changes buckets, focus remains on that card’s control and an `aria-live`
  message announces card statement, old bucket, and new bucket
- ZIP/radius validation errors are programmatically associated, announced, and do not
  erase input
- one-card mobile discovery exposes round position and preserves reactions when moving
  forward/back
- 320 CSS-pixel reflow, 400% zoom, reduced motion, forced colors, and minimum 44×44
  CSS-pixel touch targets for every interactive control
- automated axe checks plus manual VoiceOver/Safari and NVDA/Chrome passes for boundary,
  sort, reflection, round, evidence detail, and shelf flows

### 14. Failure and degraded states

- **No ZIP match:** let the student correct it or continue with all distance settings
  disabled.
- **Preferred radius exceeds maximum:** block continuation with a plain-language fix.
- **Too few candidates inside maximum:** explain which boundary causes the shortage and
  let the student revise it; do not silently cross it.
- **No mapped interest:** show nearby concepts and allow free exploration rather than
  hallucinating a program mapping.
- **Sparse program evidence:** label the limitation and avoid program-strength language.
- **AI unavailable:** derive preferences from cards and continue deterministically.
- **AI output invalid:** retry once with schema repair, then use the non-AI fallback.
- **All cards marked essential:** ask the student to choose the five hardest to give up.
  After confirmation, those five remain essential and the remaining cards move to
  interesting. The student may undo the batch change.
- **Contradictory reactions:** surface the tension rather than oscillating invisibly.
- **No unfamiliar schools saved after two rounds:** say the current constraints may be
  producing a narrow pool and suggest one reversible way to broaden it. Do not claim
  that every displayed school was familiar.

## Open Questions

1. Which interest family should be the first reviewed ontology lake? Environmental
   science/policy/engineering is a strong demonstration candidate but must be tested
   against the first student’s real interests.
2. Which experience cards can be supported entirely by current public data? Unsupported
   cards remain reflection-only in v1 rather than triggering editorial retrieval.
3. Should a student be able to convert an experience card into a hard exclusion during
   onboarding, or only after seeing the consequence in a discovery round?
4. Should cost become an optional hard guardrail in v1, or remain a downstream research
   dimension until a family has run official net-price calculators?
5. Should approximate distance be computed from a region-indexed ZIP centroid bundle in
   the browser or by an ephemeral server endpoint? The data spike must compare license,
   bundle size, latency, caching, and privacy before selection.

## Success Criteria

### Prototype validation

- Observe at least two high school students currently forming real lists, without
  guiding their clicks.
- The student can explain, in their own words, why each saved school appeared.
- The student changes, adds, or clarifies at least one preference after seeing a school;
  this validates the learning-loop premise.
- No student mistakes the discovery round for a ranking or admissions prediction.

The two-student prototype is qualitative. It can invalidate the interaction but cannot
establish behavioral rates.

### One-lake pilot validation

- Run 10 sessions after correcting prototype usability failures.
- In at least 6 of 10 sessions, a student-declared unfamiliar school reaches `Research
  next`.
- In at least 8 of 10 sessions with a saved school, the student can accurately restate
  a saved reason.
- Fewer than 10% of displayed reasons receive `Evidence looks wrong`; every such report
  is reviewed before broader release.

### Product quality

- 100% of recommendation reasons resolve to stored evidence IDs, source vintages, and
  limitation copy.
- 0 schools violate an absolute geography maximum in property-based candidate tests.
- AI unavailability does not prevent card sort, candidate generation, or source review.
- Card sort and reactions pass keyboard, screen-reader, 400% zoom, reduced-motion, and
  touch-target testing.
- Candidate rounds meet documented diversity constraints or explicitly report why they
  cannot.
- Suppressed or missing program outcomes never become zero values or negative signals.

### Behavioral measurement

- Track starts, card-sort completion, reflection skip/accept/edit, round completion,
  research-next saves, reason corrections, source opens, and boundary revisions.
- Do not track ZIP or free text.
- Primary metric: sessions with at least one unfamiliar school saved for research.
- Trust metric: `Wrong reason` rate by evidence type and explanation template.
- Learning metric: sessions where an explicit preference changes after a school
  reaction.
